# Utah Student Data Protection Compliance
## Utah Code 53E-9-3 Third-Party Contractor Agreement

**Last Updated: November 1, 2025**

**Applicable To:** Utah K-12 Schools, School Districts, and Educational Entities

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document outlines SLCTrips' compliance with **Utah Code Title 53E-9, Part 3 (Student Data Protection)** when acting as a third-party contractor for Utah education entities using our Guardian educational content and services.

### 1.1 When This Applies

This agreement applies when:
- A Utah K-12 school, school district, or education entity contracts with SLCTrips
- Students access SLCTrips content through an institutional account or contract
- The education entity provides or collects student data in connection with our services
- TK-000 Guardian County Profile content is used in educational settings

### 1.2 Regulatory Framework

SLCTrips complies with:
- **Utah Code §53E-9-301** - Definitions
- **Utah Code §53E-9-305** - Collecting Student Data
- **Utah Code §53E-9-309** - Third-Party Contractors
- **Administrative Rule R277-487** - Public School Data Confidentiality and Disclosure
- **FERPA** (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act)
- **COPPA** (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act)

---

## 2. Definitions (Utah Code §53E-9-301)

As used in this agreement, the following definitions from Utah Code §53E-9-301 apply:

**"Education Entity"** means:
- A school district
- A public school
- The Utah Schools for the Deaf and the Blind
- A charter school
- The State Board of Education
- The Utah State Board of Education
- Any other state education agency

**"Personally Identifiable Student Data"** includes but is not limited to:
- Student names
- Date of birth
- Address
- Email address
- Student identification numbers
- Biometric records
- Educational records
- Any information that, alone or in combination, is linked to a specific student

**"Student Data"** means:
- Data collected by an education entity
- Data about students maintained by an education entity
- Data collected or maintained by a third-party contractor on behalf of an education entity

**"Targeted Advertising"** means:
- Presenting advertisements to a student where the advertisement is selected based on information obtained or inferred over time from that student's online behavior, usage of applications, or student data
- Does NOT include advertising based on current visit to a location or response to a specific request, without retention over time

**"Third-Party Contractor"** means:
- A person who, pursuant to a contract with an education entity, collects or receives student data to provide a product or service as described in the contract
- **SLCTrips acts as a third-party contractor under this definition when contracted by Utah education entities**

## 3. Data Collection and Use Restrictions (Utah Code §53E-9-309)

### 3.1 Permitted Uses

SLCTrips shall use personally identifiable student data **STRICTLY** for the purpose of providing the contracted product or service within the negotiated contract terms.

**Permitted Purposes:**
- Providing access to TK-000 Guardian educational content
- Delivering contracted TripKit educational resources
- Providing technical support for educational services
- Fulfilling contractual obligations to the education entity
- Complying with legal requirements

### 3.2 Prohibited Uses

SLCTrips shall **NOT** use student data for:

**Targeted Advertising (Utah Code §53E-9-309(2)(a)):**
- We do NOT engage in targeted advertising to students
- We do NOT track student behavior over time to serve personalized ads
- We do NOT use student data for marketing or advertising purposes

**Secondary Use (Utah Code §53E-9-309(2)(b)):**
- Student data shall NOT be used for purposes beyond the contracted service
- Student data shall NOT be sold or shared with third parties for commercial purposes
- Student data shall NOT be used to build profiles for non-educational purposes

**Creating Student Profiles (Utah Code §53E-9-309(2)(c)):**
- We do NOT create or maintain student profiles except as necessary to provide contracted services
- We do NOT use student data for predictive analytics outside educational purposes
- We do NOT aggregate student data across education entities without explicit permission

### 3.3 Data Minimization

SLCTrips collects only the minimum student data necessary to:
- Provide educational content access
- Support student learning objectives
- Comply with contractual obligations
- Meet legal requirements

**We do NOT collect:**
- Social Security numbers (unless legally required)
- Medical or health information
- Biometric data
- Religious affiliation
- Political affiliation
- Student discipline records
- Any data not necessary for educational service delivery

## 4. Contract Provisions (Utah Code §53E-9-309)

### 4.1 Required Contract Terms

All contracts between SLCTrips and Utah education entities shall include:

**Data Deletion Provisions (§53E-9-309(3)(a)):**
- At the education entity's request, SLCTrips shall delete student data according to specified timelines
- Deletion shall be permanent and irreversible
- Confirmation of deletion shall be provided in writing

**Secondary Use Prohibition (§53E-9-309(3)(b)):**
- If required by the education entity, SLCTrips shall not use student data for secondary purposes
- Contract shall explicitly list permitted uses

**Audit Rights (§53E-9-309(3)(c)):**
- Education entities may audit SLCTrips to verify compliance with this agreement
- Audits may occur annually or upon reasonable suspicion of non-compliance
- SLCTrips shall cooperate fully and provide requested documentation

**Data Return/Destruction (§53E-9-309(4)):**
- Upon contract completion or non-renewal, SLCTrips shall:
  - Return all student data to the education entity, OR
  - Permanently delete all student data
- Exception: Student or parent provides consent for data retention
- Timeline: Within 30 days of contract end date

### 4.2 Information Security Program (§53E-9-309(5))

SLCTrips maintains a comprehensive information security program that includes:

**Administrative Safeguards:**
- Designated Data Protection Officer
- Employee training on student data protection
- Access controls and role-based permissions
- Incident response procedures
- Regular security policy reviews

**Technical Safeguards:**
- Encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Encryption of data at rest (AES-256)
- Multi-factor authentication for system access
- Regular security audits and penetration testing
- Intrusion detection and prevention systems
- Secure backup and disaster recovery

**Physical Safeguards:**
- Secure data center facilities (via Supabase)
- Environmental controls
- Access restrictions and monitoring

### 4.3 Data Breach Notification (§53E-9-309(6))

In the event of a data breach involving student data:

**Immediate Actions (within 24 hours):**
- Contain the breach and prevent further unauthorized access
- Notify the education entity's designated contact
- Document the breach circumstances

**Follow-Up Notification (within 72 hours):**
- Provide detailed written notification including:
  - Nature and scope of the breach
  - Types of student data compromised
  - Number of affected students
  - Steps taken to contain the breach
  - Recommended actions for the education entity
  - SLCTrips' remediation plan

**Ongoing Obligations:**
- Cooperate with education entity's investigation
- Provide updates on remediation efforts
- Implement measures to prevent recurrence
- Comply with any legal notification requirements

## 5. Student and Parent Rights

### 5.1 Access Rights

Students and parents have the right to:
- Access student data collected by SLCTrips
- Request copies of student records
- Review how student data is used
- Understand third-party data sharing (none for marketing purposes)

**Request Process:**
- Submit written request to education entity or SLCTrips
- Verification of identity required
- Response within 30 days

### 5.2 Correction Rights

Students and parents may:
- Request correction of inaccurate student data
- Challenge data accuracy
- Request updates to outdated information

**Correction Process:**
- Submit correction request with supporting documentation
- SLCTrips reviews and responds within 15 business days
- If disputed, education entity makes final determination

### 5.3 Deletion Rights

Students and parents may request:
- Deletion of student data upon contract completion
- Removal of specific data elements (subject to legal requirements)
- Account closure and data purge

**Deletion Process:**
- Request submitted to education entity
- Education entity directs SLCTrips to delete
- Permanent deletion completed within 30 days
- Written confirmation provided

### 5.4 Opt-Out Rights

Parents may opt-out of:
- Specific data collection practices (subject to functional limitations)
- Non-essential features requiring additional data
- Future use of student work samples or submissions

**Note:** Some data collection may be necessary for core service functionality and cannot be opted out while using the service.

## 6. Data Retention and Destruction

### 6.1 Retention Periods

**During Active Contract:**
- Student data retained only as long as necessary for educational purposes
- Inactive student accounts purged after 12 months of inactivity (or as specified in contract)
- Regular data audits to identify and remove unnecessary data

**After Contract Termination:**
- All student data returned or deleted within 30 days unless:
  - Student/parent provides written consent for retention
  - Legal hold or litigation requires preservation
  - State or federal law mandates retention

### 6.2 Destruction Methods

**Digital Data:**
- Secure deletion using industry-standard methods (DOD 5220.22-M or equivalent)
- Multi-pass overwriting of storage media
- Cryptographic erasure of encrypted data
- Verification of complete deletion

**Backup Data:**
- Deletion from all backup systems
- Tape destruction (if applicable)
- Cloud backup purging with verification

**Documentation:**
- Certificate of destruction provided to education entity
- Detailed accounting of data deleted
- Audit trail maintained for compliance verification

- SLCTrips does NOT sell student data

## 8. Educational Use Provisions

### 8.1 TK-000 Guardian Content

**Free Educational Content:**
- TK-000 Guardian County Profiles are free educational resources
- No student account required for general classroom use
- Teachers may use content without data collection

**Optional Student Accounts:**
- If education entity requests student accounts for tracking/progress:
  - Minimal data collected (first name, grade level, classroom identifier)
  - Data used only for educational progress tracking
  - No personally identifiable information required beyond what's necessary

### 8.2 Classroom Usage Data

**Aggregate Analytics Only:**
- SLCTrips may collect aggregate, anonymized classroom usage data:
  - Content viewed
  - Time spent on educational materials
  - Completion rates
  - Learning objective achievement

**No Individual Tracking:**
- Aggregate data cannot identify individual students
- Data used to improve educational content quality
- Reports provided to education entities show class-level trends only

### 8.3 Student-Created Content

**Ownership:**
- Students retain ownership of work created using SLCTrips tools
- Education entity retains educational use rights
- SLCTrips does NOT claim ownership of student work

**Use Restrictions:**
- Student work NOT used for marketing without explicit consent
- Student work NOT shared outside education entity without permission
- Anonymous student work samples may be used for educational research with parent consent
